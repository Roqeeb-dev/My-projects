Recipe for cooking beans

Description

A beans recipe is a hearty and nutritious dish that serves as a versatile staple in many cuisines worldwide. This recipe features tender, slow-cooked beans infused with rich flavors from onions, garlic, and a blend of spices, creating a comforting and satisfying meal. Whether prepared as a standalone dish, paired with rice, or used as a filling for tacos and wraps, beans offer a high-protein, fiber-rich option that’s both delicious and budget-friendly. Perfect for a weeknight dinner or a weekend feast, this recipe can be easily customized with herbs, vegetables, or meats to suit your taste.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups dried beans (black beans, pinto beans, or kidney beans)
  • 6 cups water (for soaking and cooking)
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 medium onion, finely chopped
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 1/2 teaspoon chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 bay leaf

Steps

  1. Soak the Beans Rinse the dried beans under cold water to remove any dirt or debris. Place them in a large bowl, cover with water, and let them soak overnight (8–12 hours). Drain and rinse before cooking.
  2. Cook the Beans In a large pot, add the soaked beans and 6 cups of water. Bring to a boil over medium-high heat, then reduce the heat to low. Add the bay leaf and simmer for 1–1.5 hours, or until the beans are tender. Drain the beans and set them aside, discarding the bay leaf.
  3. Prepare the Seasoning Base Heat olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and sauté until softened, about 3–4 minutes. Stir in the minced garlic, cumin, smoked paprika, and chili powder (if using), cooking for 1 minute until fragrant.
  4. Combine Beans with Seasoning Add the cooked beans to the skillet along with the diced tomatoes (if using). Stir well to combine and allow the mixture to simmer for 10 minutes, letting the flavors meld. Season with salt and black pepper to taste.
  5. Serve and Garnish Remove the skillet from heat and garnish with fresh cilantro, if desired. Serve the beans warm as a main dish, side dish, or filling for tacos and wraps. Enjoy! -->
